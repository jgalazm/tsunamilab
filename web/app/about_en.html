Tsunami-Lab

What is it ?

Tsunami Lab is an educational platform enabling simulation and display of tsunamis. Contrarily to other frameworks, such as National Ocean and Atmosphere Administration (NOAA, USA), Tsunami-Lab includes historical tsunami scenarios and offers the possibility to configure new ones.

What is a tsunami?

A tsunami corresponds to a series a waves generated in the ocean, propagating fast in all directions from its origin, immediately after a submarine sismic perturbation (earthquake, volcanic eruption, meteorite fall, etc.) that initiate a vertical movement of the water column. The propagation speed and the wave altitude are widely altered in the vicinity of the coast, leading to a wide energy dumping characterized by a tremendously destructive power.

*Source: http://www.onemi.cl/tsunami/

Motivations

  • Create a useful tool
  • Produce a high quality software
  • Train people thanks to experimentation

Vision

Tsunami-Lab intends to become a reference tool for tsunamis (both for kids and adults) everywhere in the world. It should help people understand - thanks to an interactive lab - how tsunamis propagate and may cause casualties.

Our next steps
